# Recent Swedish Migration Rule Changes - June 2026

Purpose: dated rule-change snapshot for LexNordic Migration Board.

Last checked: 2026-06-12

This document is not legal advice. It is a build-time source note for a hackathon prototype that prepares AI consultation packets.

## Why This Matters

Several Swedish migration rules changed in June 2026. The MVP is a fictional rejected work-permit appeal, so the agents must distinguish:

- rules that are already in force,
- transitional rules,
- exemptions,
- proposed future changes,
- case law that may have been affected by new statutes or ordinance changes.

## Work Permits: In Force From 1 June 2026

Official sources:

- Migrationsverket news, 2026-06-01: https://www.migrationsverket.se/nyheter/news-archive/2026-06-01-new-rules-for-work-permits-now-apply.html
- Migrationsverket salary requirement page: https://www.migrationsverket.se/en/word-explanations/salary-requirements-for-a-work-permit.html
- Migrationsverket occupation exemptions, 2026-05-25: https://www.migrationsverket.se/en/news-archive/news/2026-05-25-new-rules-for-labour-immigration---these-occupations-are-exempted.html
- Government press release, 2026-01-13: https://www.government.se/press-releases/2026/01/new-minimum-wage-level-for-labour-immigration-and-stricter-requirements-for-family-member-immigration/

Key changes:

- New salary requirement for most work permits: at least 90 percent of Sweden's median salary at the time of application.
- Current published level: SEK 33,390 per month.
- Salary must still be in line with Swedish collective agreements or common practice in the profession or industry.
- The new requirement applies to first-time work-permit applicants who receive a decision on or after 1 June 2026, even if they applied before 1 June.
- Extension transition: people granted a current work permit under the old rules and applying for extension from 1 June through 1 December 2026 are assessed under the old "good living" maintenance requirement instead. The official page states the old level as 80 percent of median salary, currently SEK 29,680 per month.
- From 2 December 2026, the new salary requirement applies to extensions too.
- If the stay is for a maximum of one year, applicants must show that they have or have applied for comprehensive health insurance.
- The Swedish Migration Agency may reject applications due to shortcomings linked to the employer, including certain crimes, suspected offences, or sanctions.

## Work-Permit Exemptions And Exclusions

Some occupations and applicant groups are exempt from the 90 percent salary threshold. If exempt, salary must instead be at least 75 percent of Sweden's median salary at the time of application.

The official exemption list includes, among others:

- chemistry/chemical engineering technicians,
- laboratory engineers,
- IT operations/support/systems/network technicians,
- several assistant-nurse and care roles,
- childcare assistants,
- farm-animal/crop and forestry roles,
- welders, structural steel workers, maintenance mechanics,
- electrical distribution technicians,
- butchers and food-industry machine operators,
- agricultural and forestry machinery drivers,
- berry pickers and planters, with an important caveat below.

Additional exempt applicant groups include:

- former students who may apply for a work permit from within Sweden,
- people with foreign qualifications seeking Swedish licence-related employment as pharmacist, doctor, nurse, or dentist,
- people with or after temporary protection under the EU Temporary Protection Directive who apply based on employment,
- employees at certain tech or life-science startup companies that meet the official criteria.

Excluded occupations:

- Forest berry picker under Chapter 6, Section 2 of the Aliens Act.
- Personal assistant.

Important caveat: Migrationsverket states that a forest berry picker cannot get a normal work permit under Chapter 6, Section 2 of the Aliens Act. A berry picker working for an employer established in Sweden may instead apply for a seasonal work permit.

## Seasonal Work, ICT, And EU Blue Cards

Official source:

- Migrationsverket work-permit news, 2026-06-01: https://www.migrationsverket.se/nyheter/news-archive/2026-06-01-new-rules-for-work-permits-now-apply.html

Changes:

- Seasonal work salaries must correspond at least to the full-time minimum under Swedish collective agreements or common practice, even for part-time seasonal work.
- ICT permit compensation follows the same full-time minimum principle.
- EU Blue Card salary threshold is not changed by these June rules. Migrationsverket separately states the EU Blue Card threshold as SEK 52,000 per month since 9 July 2025.
- EU Blue Card maximum permit period increases from two years to four years at a time.
- Seasonal work maximum period increases from six to nine months within a twelve-month period.

## Employer Notification Requirement

Official source:

- Migrationsverket occupation exemptions, 2026-05-25: https://www.migrationsverket.se/en/news-archive/news/2026-05-25-new-rules-for-labour-immigration---these-occupations-are-exempted.html

Employers with an employee holding a work permit in Sweden must notify Migrationsverket if the employee has not started working within four months from the date the permit came into effect.

Information from employer-related suspicion and criminal-record registers may be disclosed to Migrationsverket in work-permit cases.

## Students And Researchers: In Force From 11 June 2026

Official sources:

- Migrationsverket higher-education studies news, 2026-05-25: https://www.migrationsverket.se/nyheter/news-archive/2026-05-25-new-rules-for-residence-permits-for-studies-in-higher-education.html
- Migrationsverket researchers and doctoral students news, 2026-05-25: https://www.migrationsverket.se/nyheter/news-archive/2026-05-25-new-rules-for-researchers-and-doctoral-students.html

Higher-education changes:

- First- and second-cycle students may generally work a maximum of 15 hours per week during semesters.
- Work is unrestricted during June, July, and August.
- Students who completed a programme of at least two semesters and have a diploma/certificate may work without limitation while they still have the permit, provided they are not applying for a new first- or second-cycle study permit.
- It becomes harder to switch from a study permit to certain other permits from within Sweden. For work permits, the starting point is completion of at least two semesters.
- Rules apply to decisions from 11 June 2026 onward. People granted study residence permits before 11 June 2026 are not affected by the 15-hour limit, increased study-progress requirement, or address-notification obligation until they apply to extend.

Researcher and doctoral-student changes:

- Doctoral students with a hosting agreement can apply under research-permit rules.
- More people can apply for research or doctoral-study permits from within Sweden.
- Post-research or post-doctoral job-search permits can run for at least twelve months and up to eighteen months.
- Permanent residence may be available after three consecutive years with a qualifying research, doctoral, research-mobility, or Swedish EU Blue Card permit, subject to other requirements.

## Temporary Protection Directive: In Force From 11 June 2026

Official sources:

- Migrationsverket news, 2026-06-11: https://www.migrationsverket.se/nyheter/news-archive/2026-06-11-new-rules-for-people-from-ukraine-from-11-june.html
- Migrationsverket news, 2026-05-25: https://www.migrationsverket.se/nyheter/news-archive/2026-05-25-new-rules-for-applying-for-another-residence-permit-from-within-sweden.html

People holding residence permits under the EU Temporary Protection Directive may, if they meet the relevant requirements, apply from within Sweden for:

- employment,
- self-employment,
- research,
- higher education studies,
- EU Blue Card.

Changing permit basis may affect support, housing, daily allowance, and access to other benefits. Agents must surface this as a risk note, not as a recommendation.

## EU Migration And Asylum Pact: In Force From 12 June 2026

Official sources:

- Migrationsverket news, 2026-06-12: https://www.migrationsverket.se/en/news-archive/news/2026-06-12-common-migration-rules-introduced-for-eu-member-states.html
- Migrationsverket asylum applicant notice, 2026-06-12: https://www.migrationsverket.se/nyheter/news-archive/2026-06-12-the-migration-pact-is-introduced-on-the-12-june.html
- Migrationsverket explainer, updated 2026-04-29: https://www.migrationsverket.se/en/about-the-swedish-migration-agency/the-swedish-migration-agency-answers/2025/2025-06-18-what-is-the-new-eu-migration-and-asylum-pact-about.html
- Migrationsverket application-card notice, 2026-06-12: https://www.migrationsverket.se/en/employers/news-archive-for-employers/news/2026-06-12-application-cards-are-introduced-in-connection-with-the-migration-pact.html

Key changes:

- The EU Pact on Migration and Asylum applies across the EU from 12 June 2026.
- Sweden applies a common legal framework for management and reception of people seeking international protection.
- Screening at external borders includes identity, health, vulnerability, and security checks.
- Border procedures may accelerate cases assessed as unlikely to qualify for international protection.
- Migrationsverket says the Pact aims to make procedures more efficient and shorten wait times.
- New application cards are introduced for people who apply for international protection from 12 June 2026 onward.
- People who applied for asylum before 12 June 2026 are not affected in the same way. Existing LMA cards remain during a transition period and will be replaced during summer/autumn 2026.

MVP implication: asylum cases are more procedurally sensitive after 12 June 2026. Keep asylum out of the hackathon MVP except as a risk-escalation path.

## Track Change Abolished: In Force From 1 April 2025

Official source:

- Migrationsverket news, 2025-03-13: https://www.migrationsverket.se/en/news-archive/news/2025-03-13-track-change-abolished-and-rules-on-statutory-limitation-period-changed.html

Rules:

- The possibility of changing track from rejected asylum application to work-permit basis was abolished from 1 April 2025.
- The change entered into force without transitional provisions.
- Existing permits obtained through track change generally cannot be extended on that basis.
- Migrationsverket estimated around 4,700 people were affected.
- Deportation/expulsion decisions now remain valid for five years from the date the person leaves Sweden and the relevant territory, rather than four years from when the decision entered into force.
- Migrationsverket may decide longer re-entry bans in some situations.

MVP implication: if fictional facts include prior rejected asylum, track-change history, return decision, or re-entry ban, the Risk agent must block autonomous completion and return an explicit high-sensitivity risk flag.

## Proposed / Not Yet Stable For MVP Rules

Official source:

- Migrationsverket regulation-change explainer, 2026-04-29: https://www.migrationsverket.se/en/about-the-swedish-migration-agency/the-swedish-migration-agency-answers/2026/2026-04-29-how-have-migration-regulations-changed.html

Track these, but do not hard-code them as final unless verified again:

- Return-related changes proposed to enter into force on 13 July 2026, including broader internal immigration checks and expanded biometric use.
- A new Reception of Asylum Seekers Act entering into force on 1 October 2026, including stricter accommodation-related duties.
- A proposal to phase out permanent residence permits in asylum-related cases, proposed to enter into force on 12 July 2026.
- Stricter citizenship changes discussed for 6 June 2026, including residence period, language/society knowledge, good conduct, and self-support requirements.
